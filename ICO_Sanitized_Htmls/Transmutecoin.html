Business solution

COLDFALL Corporation

COLDFALL Corporation is one of the pioneers to devise transmutation process for recycling carbon-based materials with Zero CO2 Emission & Waste Pollution..

The company aims to deploy a patented pyrolytic microwave conversion technique to obtain energy products and generate electricity. COLDFALL aims to leverage coal as a feedstock to achieve a fuel source of powerful electrical generators and also to provide a green source of electricity while decimating the CO2 emission.

The company will also serve as a base platform for joint ventures and strategic partnerships with existing recycling facilities within targeted licensed market areas to improve their levels of efficiency, profitability and environmental impact.

Tires

  • 1 billion automobiles were in operation as of 2010.
  • 2 billion automobiles are estimated to be in operation by 2035.
  • 4 Tires/vehicle will account for the disposal of 8 billion tires.
  • EPA Estimate
    • Two to three billion used tires are discarded with a growth pace of 320 million tires per year.
    • Despite rubber & energy companies coming up with alternative solutions for reusing old tires, fewer than 5% are actually being recycled.
  • In the US almost 320 million tires are replaced each year. 70% (225 million tires) are disposed of directly into the environment, 38 million tires are dumped on the roadside, while 50 million are disposed of in landfills.
  • Tires & pollution control: the State of Texas has set aside $1.5 billion for environmental clean-up, while the City of Houston has set aside $175 million.
  • Over 72 GW of electricity generating capacity is set to retire. 72 GW will constitute electricity for about 44.7 Million homes in every state of the west Mississippi river, excluding Texas
  • Coal-fired power plants will constitute 94% of these closures, shutting down over 1/5th of the United States

Plastics

  • Plastic production to date measures 8.3 billion metric tons.
  • 91% of plastic is never recycled.
  • 300 million metric tons end up in landfills.
  • Over 8 Million tons of plastic ends up in the ocean every year. 89% of this is single-use plastic.
  • 73% of the deep-water fish & shellfish in the North Atlantic Ocean mistake micro-plastics for food.
  • The current per capita consumption for polyolefins via technical & packing usage was quantified at 100 kg in 2010 and said to rise to 140 kg
  • If present trends continue, by 2050, there will be 12 billion metric tons of plastic in landfills

Coal

  • Coal-derived fuels could play a major role in meeting the future global energy needs
  • Current globally accepted practices include indirect liquefaction: Coal is gasified to yield CO and H2 which is used to produce liquid fuels in Fischer-Tropsch Synthesis Process.
  • A plant in Secunda utilized indirect liquefaction that converts more than 40 million metric tons of coal to liquid fuel with the implementation of Sasol Technology
  • Direct liquefaction: fuel is produced directly from coal with the addition of Hydrogen at mild temperatures but extremely high pressure which leads to high conversion cost
  • Both processes yield hydrocarbon fuels from coal at high cost, consuming a large amount of water while also producing CO2
  • COLDFALL is developing a promising alternative to the current state of the art processes while producing ZERO CO2 in the direct transmutation process.

  • The pyro-oils are converted into diesel and jet fuels for commercial sales. COLDFALL’s process eliminates air pollution which is otherwise caused by coal-fired power plants.
