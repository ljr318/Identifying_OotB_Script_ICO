Ways to Improve Your Brain Health

Several claims have been made about how to become smarter, more intelligent or healthier. But, many of these claims have no scientific basis, and most have them do not have any empirical evidence to support them. In light of this, leading online prescription pharmacy MedicineDirect.co.uk have given you ten ways to improve your overall brain, mental and welbeing health.

1. Stimulate your brain – Several kinds of research later, scientists have found that brain activities stimulate brain cells and help generate new ones. This develops neurological plasticity and builds up a functional reserve that provides for future cell losses. Any activity that stimulates the brain enables you to make your brain stronger. Some ways you can do this are by reading, solving puzzles or math problems.

2. Physical exercise – Research suggests that physical exercise is beneficial to your brain. Exercise increases your heartbeat, which results in an increased flow of blood and oxygen to your brain. This enhances the functions of the brain. It also strengthens and improves the connection between the brain cells (synaptic gaps). This makes the brain more efficient and adaptive, which ultimately results in better performance.

3. Improve your diet – A nutrition-filled, healthy and balanced diet can be extremely beneficial for the body as well as your brain. For instance, people who have a diet that includes fruits, vegetables, nuts, fish, unsaturated fats etc. are less likely to develop cognitive impairments.

4. Improve your blood pressure – High blood pressure among adults increases the risk of decline in cognitive functions during old age. Therefore, it is essential that you maintain a lifestyle that keeps your blood pressure levels maintained.

5. Improving blood sugar levels – Diabetes is a significant risk factor and cause for dementia. And, as well all know, diabetes is a lifestyle disorder; it is essential to eat right and exercise regularly.

6. Improve your cholesterol levels – High levels of cholesterol can be associated with increased risk of dementia. Hence, diet, lifestyle, weight control and exercise can go a long way in improving your cholesterol levels.

7. Limit your alcohol consumption – Excessive drinking can pose a significant threat when it comes to neurodevelopmental functions in kids as well as neurological functions in adults. For a healthier brain and higher cognitive functionality, limit your alcohol consumption.

8. Stay positive – People who are anxious, sleep-deprived, depressed or exhausted often do poorly when it comes to cognitive functions. Poor cognitive functions do not necessarily mean an unhealthy brain, but it is advisable to maintain good mental health and experience positive emotions.

The Top Benefits of Regular Exercise

Exercise is absolutely essential to live a healthy life. Being active has impressive health benefits, physically and mentally. There are many types of physical activities such as swimming , jogging, running, walking, dancing etc. We have got you a list of benefits that regular exercise has on you, both mentally and physically.

1. It makes you feel happier – Exercise has been known to improve your mood and reduce feelings of anxiety and stress. It produces changes in the part of the brain that regulates stress and anxiety. Moreover, exercise also increases the production of endorphins, that creates positive feelings and reduce pain perception.

2. Exercise aids weight loss – We all know that a sedentary lifestyle and inactivity is one of the primary cause for weight gain and obesity. Regular exercise increases the metabolic rate, which burns off more calories and helps you lose weight faster. Aerobic exercise and resistance training combines can maximize fat loss and maintain muscle mass.

3. Exercise is beneficial for muscles and bones – Exercise plays a significant role in maintaining and building strong bones and muscles. Physical exercise can stimulate muscle gain if complemented by proper protein intake. This is because physical activity releases hormones that promote your muscles to absorb amino acids. This ensures that they grow and do not breakdown. Most importantly, exercise also helps develop higher bone density which is extremely crucial.

4. Physical activities increase energy levels – Engaging in regular exercise can boost your energy while making you feel incredibly active and fresh. Physical exercise reduces feelings of fatigue and exhaustion. They can have a significant effect on energy levels.

5. Exercise reduces the risk of chronic diseases – Lack of a healthy lifestyle is one of the major causes of chronic diseases. Regular physical activity has shown to have a lot of positive impact on health, including people suffering from chronic diseases. It improves cardiovascular fitness, body composition, insulin sensitivity, while decreases blood fat levels and blood pressure.


6. Regular exercise helps brain health and memory – Regular physical activity has been known to improve brain function and promote memory and thinking skills. It increases your heart rate, which results in an increased flow of blood and oxygen to the brain. Interestingly, they also stimulate the production of hormones responsible for the growth of brain cells. Exercise has also been seen to cause an increase in the size of the hippocampus. Hippocampus that part of the brain which is responsible for memory and learning. Lastly, exercise also causes a reduction in the changes in the brain that cause Alzheimer’s and schizophrenia.
