A brilliant opportunity to make the fashion world swirl a little better

One trend that is negatively impacting the fashion industry is the proliferation of counterfeits and pirated goods everywhere. The emergence and growth of e-commerce platforms (Amazon, Alibaba, eBay) is one of the chief reasons for the constant rise in counterfeiting. Last year, losses incurred by luxury brands from the sale of counterfeit goods via the Internet amounted to more than 30 billion USD.

Another negative trend is the highly outdated take-make-dispose model on which today’s textile industry is built. It’s hugely wasteful and a big polluter, and produces 1.2 billion tonnes of greenhouse gas emissions annually. That’s “more than those of all international flights and maritime shipping combined.” Rising disposable income levels over the past decades means there is less need to make, do, mend and love , as it’s often cheaper and more convenient simply to buy new and discard the old.

In order to preserve our most treasured items, we need secure ways to trace and prove authenticity, origin and ownership. Yet, today’s solutions are flawed.

The Ecosystem

Once items are tagged – on any on- or offline store – they become part of the PDC ecosystem, which makes them more valuable when they’re resold some time later.

Tagging assures users the item’s heritage has been checked and authenticated. Once authenticated, forever authenticated.

Every tag on a fashion item carries a private key that Projectdresscode users can use to view and authorise ownership and grow its value by creating and sharing content about the item. Tags can be scanned and read with a smartphone using the Projectdresscode mobile application.

Whenever customer decides to sell his/her item on the Projectdresscode platform that item then remains in the loop for all to enjoy – happily, respectfully, sustainably. Waste less, love more.

Once the item is re-sold the proceeds of the sellout gets shared between the seller and the brand.

By giving the brand a share in every transaction after the initial sale we decentralise the trust – which is currently dominated exclusively by brands, marketing and distribution channels – with the help of blockchain technology and the law of code.

Increasing value of pre-owned items

Blockchain is useful for tracking the authenticity of items and their owners, which is utilised by the platform. All items available exclusively on the platform have their own codes that can be verified against the blockchain.

A crypto-enabled DRESS Tag affixed to a product delivers relevant data to the end-user and gives the buyer the possibility to own, sell or trade the rights attached to it, and pass the ownership on to the next lucky buyer.

Each time the tag is scanned, additional information will create a new block on the blockchain and will also be stored on the tag.

The great advantage of the blockchain is that each block is encrypted. The second advantage is the distributed nature of the information; in case the database is partially compromised, the remaining nodes can be used to keep the information intact and original.
