Mycro brings together users for something greater than money:
Time!

As they say time is money. Some people have an excess of money but little time, making the time they have very valuable. Others have an excess of time but little money. Time is limited and we cannot stretch five minutes into an hour when we don’t have time to mow the lawn, walk the dog and grocery shop. In today’s world, our time is controlled externally by our jobs, social life, and an abundance of daily errands. There is a high demand to outsource these tasks.

Mycro solves the following problems to give people back control over their time:

—

Mycro Protocol
the underlying magic

The Mycro Protocol acts as the confidential net- working layer for all integrated decentralized ap- plications, creating peer-to-peer (P2P) connec- tions and providing an efficient way for users to enter trustless transactions over the network. The Mycro Protocol consists of client-side APIs and services that allow connected applications to confidentially perform communication functions with other users on the network.

Learn more
With the Mycro protocol we ensure:

Complete trust
—
Decentralized rating systems and user verification options are executed by smart contracts, allowing both parties to enter a ‘trustless’ transaction. All users are verified through a implemented KYC process.
Secure payments
—
Payments are held by a decentralized, escrow smart contract while the job is performed, preventing retrieval access from either party until a specific job is complete.
Democratized ratings
—
Mycro is giving the no. 1 asset in the online world back to the user. By using IPFS, we allow users to have full control over their detailed rating information. With 100% ownership, they are free to take their ratings to other platforms.

The gig economy:
a global market for local communities

The gig economy is one of the fastest growing parts of the global labour market. While the total volume in 2018 was still around 200 billion dollars, the industry is expected to grow to over 900 billion dollars by 2025. The exponential development is typical for newly emerging market segments in the digital environment and underlines the potential that lies in the gig economy.

Learn more
Research by McKinsey shows that independent workers make up 20–30% of the working-age population. This is up to 162 million people in the US and Europe alone. However, only 6% of them are using digital platforms. This market is virtually untapped and has enormous potential to become the future of how we work.

Especially the participation of millennials in the gig economy continues to rise rapidly. According to research conducted by the University of Leeds, 67% of the people are joining the gig economy because of flexibility. This happens to be one of the most important assets Mycro has to offer.

Mycro Token (MYO)

The Mycro token powers the Mycro ecosystem.

The Mycro token is a tool to achieve a deeper engagement of a broader community of token holders, partners and contributors who are the main beneficiaries of the ecosystem‘s growth.

The token is issued as an ERC-20 token compliant to the public Ethereum blockchain. The token is classified as a utility token.

Mycro tokens are deployed and transferred within the Mycro ecosystem and the Mycro mobile application. The tokens can also be circulated outside of the network and beyond the circle of people registered in it.

– Language and version of the browser software.

(2) In addition to the previously mentioned data, we set cookies on your computer when you use our website. Cookies are small text files that are stored on your hard drive in connection with your browser. These text files transmit information to us. Cookies cannot enable programs or transmit viruses to your computer. They are used to provide the web offer as a whole in a more user-friendly and effective way.

(3) Usage of Cookies:
a) This website uses the following types of cookies, their scope and functionality are explained below:
– Transient Cookies (see b)
– Persistent Cookies (see c).

b) Transient cookies are automatically deleted when you close your browser. This includes in particular the session cookies. These store a so-called Session-IDs, with which different requests of your browser of the common assign session are connected. This allows your computer to be recognized if you return to our website. Session cookies are deleted when you log out or close your browser.

c) Persistent cookies are automatically deleted after a specified period, which may vary depending on the cookie. You can delete cookies in your browser's security settings at any time.

d) You can configure your browser settings according to your wishes and e.g. refuse the acceptance of third party cookies or all cookies. Please note that you may not be able to use all functions of this website.

§ 5 Newsletter
(1) With your consent you can subscribe to our newsletter, with which we will send you information about our MYCRO.jobs project, products and services and especially about the upcoming initial coin offering and the Mycro application. The above mentioned information is available in the declaration of consent.

(2) For the subscription to our newsletter we use the so-called double-opt-in-procedure. This means that after your registration we will send you an e-mail to the provided e-mail address, in which we ask you to confirm that you wish to receive the newsletter. If you do not receive the conformation for your registration within 24 hours, your information will be blocked and automatically deleted after one month. In addition, we store the IP-addresses you use in each case and the time of registration and confirmation. The purpose of the processing activity is to prove your registration and, if necessary, to resolve a possible misuse of your personal data.

(3) The only mandatory information for sending the newsletter is your e-mail address. After your confirmation we will save your e-mail address for the purpose of sending you the newsletter. The legal basis is Art. 6 para. 1 sentence 1 a) GDPR.

(4) You can withdraw your consent to receiving the newsletter and unsubscribe from the newsletter at any time. You can withdraw you consent by clicking on the link provided in each newsletter e-mail, by sending an e-mail to [email protected] or by sending a message to the contact details given in the imprint.


§ 6 Objection or revocation to the processing of your data
(1) If you have given your consent to the processing of your data, you may revoke this consent at any time. Such a revocation influences the legitimacy of the processing of your personal data after you have submitted it to us.

(2) Insofar as we process your personal data on the basis of overweighting interests, you can submit an objection against the processing. This is the case, in particular, if the processing is not necessary for the performance of a contract that we concluded with you, which is described by us within the description of each functions. If such an objection is exercised, we ask you to please explain the reasons for which we should not collect your personal data as carried out. In the event of your justified objection, we will examine the situation and will either stop or adapt the processing of your data or inform you about our compelling reasons worthy of protection, on the basis of which we can continue the processing.
