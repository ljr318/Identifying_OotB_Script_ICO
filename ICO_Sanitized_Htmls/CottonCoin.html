A Farmer Commits Suicide Every 30 Minutes; Here's How You Can Stop The Next One.

The Cotton Coin project is a humanitarian effort created to give hope and save the lives of third world cotton farmers. Your investment in Cotton Coin will help transform the corrupt cotton industry while earning an attractive reward.

A very serious problem lies beneath all the beauty and glamour, expensive clothes, and  famous designers; more than 300,000 cotton farmers in 3rd world countries have committed suicide since 1995.

A study by the International Food Policy Research Institute identified indebtedness and hopelessness as the main reason for farmer suicide.

Lack of reliable credit,  changes in government policies, cropping patterns, pesticides exposure, and even shifts in the crops planted on the farm are contributing factors in the cause of farmer suicide.

Giving Up Their Lives for White Gold

Cotton, also known as white gold, has become the most in-demand commodity due to the insatiable appetite for cotton in the clothing and bedding industries..

Cotton is the world’s most commonly used natural fiber and is in nearly 40 percent of our clothing.

Globally, this crop covers just 2.4% of the world’s cultivated land yet it is one of the most chemically dependent crops in the world. It accounts for 24% and 11% of the global sales of insecticide and pesticides respectively – more than any other single crop.

20 million tonnes of cotton are produced each year in around 90 different countries; China, United States, India, Pakistan, Uzbekistan and Brazil account for over 75% of the global production.

Plight of Third World Cotton Farmers

For over two hundred years, the cotton industry has been plagued by various problems: poor working conditions,

Non/delayed payment to farmers, poor quality cotton,environmental pollution, lack of government support, corruption and more.

This has continued to decline with time as global warming and ozone depletion has made the weather highly unpredictable. All these factors affect the quality, consistency and sufficiency of the crop.

The cotton industry is threatened by lawsuits, corruption and scams that even data coming from third party traceability system is viewed with suspicion by participants.

This led us to wonder how we can bring back trust in cotton related information. We needed a technology which ensures that the cotton related data cannot be corrupted or modified in any way, by any party.

Blockchain Technology to the Rescue

Thanks to Satoshi Nakamoto, the anonymous creator of blockchain, we now have the most appropriate solution: Cotton Coin .

Cotton Coin runs on blockchain protocol that creates a trusted transaction ledger to integrate consumers, supply chain participants and authorities in one ecosystem. This peer-to-peer payment network can be used as an alternative to fiat currency for instant payment.

Cotton Coin also has a backend growers-to-buyers traceability solution, to be used by over 6,000+ companies, managing more than 400,000 transactions each day and reaching 30 million people through the world with the help of our agents and distributors. A front-end B2B dApp helps to authenticate cotton sources, tracks delivery and monitors cotton prices while a B2C dApp assists consumers in buying cotton directly.

Once ready, our versatile solution will help to improve the distribution of cotton, resolve procurement issues, fight off corruption, support fair trade, and build trust among the cotton supply chain companies, consumers, and authorities in the emerging markets. And most importantly, help to save the lives of cotton farmers.

We believe that building a self-sustainable business is better than any type of government aid.
