Aerospace

NAFEN™ modified bonding agents, which are widely used in laminated composites (based on carbon and glass fibers), demonstrate enhancements of critical mechanical properties by 20-50%, which could result in a significant weight reduction for finished goods made of composite materials used in aerospace.
The modified composites demonstrate improvements in Interlaminar Shear Strength, Facing Stress, Tensile Strength, Tensile Modulus, Bending Stress, Fracture Toughness K1C, and other mechanical properties. The added strength and light weight of NAFEN™ enhanced composites will help to reduce weight, improve fuel efficiency and better overall performance of the aircraft to be produced. A new generation of aircraft that consists of more than 50% composite materials (by weight) would be able to significantly reduce its total fully constructed weight.

discover more on nafen.eu

Automotive

The use of NAFEN™ solutions for the manufacture of vehicles will show benefits such as lighter but stronger materials (better fuel consumption and increased safety), reduced environmental impact, improved and miniaturized electronic systems and better economies (longer service life; lower component failure rate, etc.).
Today, an average vehicle consists of at least 130 kg of plastics and composites, according to the American Composites Manufacturers Association. Based on the expected mechanical property improvements in composites and plastics with NAFEN™ enhancement, you might expect up to a 10% weight savings per vehicle. This will result in average vehicle weight decrease and help reduce CO2 emissions. Currently, ANF Technology is providing customers with a series of products for use in automotive applications using such processes as Injection Molding, Compression Molding, Sheet Molding Compounding, Resin Transfer Molding, and more.

discover more on nafen.eu

Marine

ANF Technology is involved in industrial collaborations associated with innovative processes in yacht production, sailing mast production, and marine protective coatings. The next generation of yachts, cruisers and racing vessels will be lighter and stronger when made with NAFEN™ modified composites, and commercial vessels will be well protected against corrosion. Such materials will perfectly stand up to the extremes of marine environments.
Our solutions for Marine systems include enhancers for production of laminated composites, composite sandwich panels, adhesives, and coating modifiers. These solutions have been approved by industrial partners and guarantee the transfer of NAFEN™’s unique properties into the final products.

discover more on nafen.eu

Petroleum Industry (Oil & Gas)

The environmental pressure and industry demands that petroleum companies experience today are forcing the oil & gas industry to explore innovative solutions for the optimization of costs and implementation of ‘Cleantech’ processes.
NAFEN™ has enormous potential in various petroleum disciplines from Oil & Gas drilling, to the Production and Processing/Refining of petrochemicals. NAFEN™ offers superior corrosion resistance via enhancement of widely used anti-corrosion coatings used on oil-production equipment (pipes, drills, plugs, etc), thus reducing maintenance costs and down-time. Also, a special modification of the nanofiber structure makes it possible to use NAFEN™ as an effective adsorbing material. Moreover, in a similar way, it could provide novel approaches to improved catalytic production processes by boosting certain chemical reactions and reinforcing catalyst carriers.

discover more on nafen.eu

Sustainable Energy

NAFEN™ enhanced materials have strong potential in several segments of the energy sector. In some cases, traditional materials could be replaced by novel NAFEN™ enhanced materials. In others, the use of NAFEN™ solutions will make the finished goods more durable, thus saving material and cost over the product’s lifetime. All of these can provide significant economic and environmental benefits (less waste, smaller carbon footprint, greater energy yield, etc.).

discover more on nafen.eu

Infrastructure /Civil engineering

The outstanding chemical and physical properties of NAFEN™ enable applications in the area of Civil Engineering, ranging from the structural reinforcement of materials to the reduction of environmental impact during the construction lifecycle. Since concrete is the leading material in infrastructure applications—its stiffness, strength, and cost play a key role in modern Civil Engineering. NAFEN™ nanofibers are being tested for increasing the rate of strength gain in ultra-high performance cement mixtures.
The dispersion of specifically-functionalized Nafen™ nanofibers could be used to improve hydrophobic resistance in industrial coating systems. This can dramatically increase the life-span of concrete structures—especially flooring—as the main cause of degradation is due to exposure of the surface to the elements.

discover more on nafen.eu

Electronics

Today’s electronic devices have become smaller in size and deliver more power than ever before, which means that they typically run at higher temperatures. The main issue affecting such devices is removing that excess heat from the sensitive components since performance is only maintained when the heat is efficiently dissipated. Thermally conductive potting compounds and encapsulates are extremely important materials in that effort to control heat build-up in an electronic assembly. The formulations based on epoxy, urethane or silicone matrices are used to bond heat sinks, encapsulate power supplies and individual components. The addition of NAFEN™ nanofibers can improve the thermal conductivity of potting compounds and encapsulates by establishing heat conducting networks in a chosen matrix.
The electronic components can also be very sensitive to the impact of electromagnetic impulses. For such sensitive electronics or sensors, NAFEN™ can provide increased dielectric insulating properties in the materials used to encase the electronics, or in the films, adhesives and coatings used to encapsulate them.

discover more on nafen.eu

Industrial catalysis

NAFEN™ can be used as a catalyst and/or support for the active component of a catalytic process. Catalyst carriers reinforced with NAFEN™ substantially improve several mechanical characteristics of the catalyst, thus allowing their use in industrial processes at harsh physical and chemical conditions. NAFEN™ nanofibers can also enhance the catalytic performance of the materials due to NAFEN™’s high aspect ratio, high specific surface area, as well as the special organization of the structure, such as pore size distribution, the degree of crystallinity, phase composition, and surface chemistry.
