Frequently Asked Questions

What is cryptocurrency?

Cryptocurrency is an innovative system of currency. While regular currencies (USD, EUR, YEN, etc.), referred to as FIAT, are controlled by a central agency, cryptocurrencies are decentralized. This means that no one has control over the emittance and circulation of the currency. Furthermore, transactions in the crypto world happen non-stop (no bank hours) and they are confirmed through the blockchain by miners. This way all transactions are public and transparent. For more information seek advice online.

What is the difference between Bitcoin, Ethereum, etc.?

There are multiple blockchain offerings. While Bitcoin is the most recognisable due to its early entrance into the market, other blockchains are trying to expand the crypto world either by introducing new uses or by improving on existing ones. Ethereum is quicker and cheaper to transact than Bitcoin. Furthermore, it offers the ability to launch new tokens on the market using their ERC20 smart contract algorithm.

What is the SAVE token?

The SAVE token is an ERC20 token. This will be the basis of the SAVE service offering. Once launched on the market, following the ICO, SAVE will be available on major exchanges (e.g. Yobit, Poloniex, Binance, Bitfinex, etc.*).
As a result, owners of SAVE (from the ICO stages) will be able to sell their tokens to other investors, to SAVE service seekers, or to purchase themselves the SAVE service.
* Subject to negotiation with different exchanges

What is an ICO?

ICO or Initial Coin Offering is a method of launching a crypto token on the market. During the ICO an investor buys the token at a low, preferential price, with the expectation of extraordinary profits once the token is launched publicly on exchanges. In return, the issuer raises funding to develop, improve, launch the real service behind the ICO. In a sense, an ICO is similar to IPO (Initial Public Offering),
A successful ICO provides a good product or service.

What is the value proposition of SAVE?

We want to cover a niche in the sense that we identify 2 major categories of investors into cryptocurrency:
The investors who trade: these investors are looking for quick profits, they trade multiple times a week, some even use bots. These wouldn’t normally be interested in our service; however, they would take advantage of trading our token on the market. They are the ones we recommend buying SAVE during the ICO phases.
The investors who “hodl”: These are the investors our service is aimed at. Our market analysis shows that a large potential investor group is reticent to investing in cryptocurrency because of the risks associated with the crypto market.
Hence, our service aims to attract the latter investors into the market. They know that they can buy their favourite, approved, coins and ensure that if the market collapses, they can claim their initial investment back. If all goes well, they will benefit of an appreciated-in-value coin, i.e. SAVE.
To summarise, if the market collapses, the investors gets his investment back minus the policy cost (FIAT + SAVE); if the market does not collapse, the investor keeps his protected coins and his SAVE part of the policy cost.
