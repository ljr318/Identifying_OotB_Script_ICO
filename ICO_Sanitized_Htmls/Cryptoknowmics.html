FAQS

Please find below answers to some of the frequently asked questions (FAQs). If you have any other questions, please get in touch.

What is an ICO?

ICO stands for "Initial Coin Offering". It is an innovative form of efficient and simple fund raising. An ICO is similar to an IPO in the stock market. Any individual or a company can easily ask for funds by the public or even become an investor himself/itself. Every ICO has two compulsory aspects blockchain controlled transactions and a predetermined amount of new crypto unit (token/coin) has to be issued.

Is it safe to invest in an ICO?

Yes, it is safe to invest in an ICO, but one must definitely research about the background of the projects the ICO is for. An investor should be fully sure about the authenticity of the ICO before investing.

Is an ICO legal?

The legality of ICOs depend on the country. For example - it is legal in USA and is illegal in Bangladesh. As long as the ICO holder doesnt mislead his users and investors, it is legal, provided it has all the documentation and all of it has been approved by a proper legal counsel. It is legal for everyone involved if the ICO doesnt misrepresent their coin/token and the investors follow all the rules and regulations of their country.

What are tokens?

A token or a coin is a cryptocurrency unit issued during an ICO. An ICO holder can easily raise funds to kickstart his/her project or even crowdfund other projects. A fixed amount of tokens/coins are issued by every ICO to be used as a probable source of profit once the ICO goes up on exchanges and/or as the capital used for the project.

What is a whitepaper?

A whitepaper provides a comprehensive and complete summary about the project which includes detailed information about its various ventures, the benefits it will provide to its users, subscribers and investors, features of the initiative, team behind the project and the future plans of the project.

How do ICOs work?

ICO is like a fundraising event, where an individual or company produces and sells tokens in exchange of fiat currency from the public (investors). The individual or company issuing the tokens determine the amount they will raise from the ICO. The launching of an ICO involves a certain procedure which starts with thinking of an innovative idea and mention it to the community. Then the white paper is made which contains features and detailed description of the project and then the projects is advertised to the public.

What happens to the tokens which are left unsold?

If some tokens are left unsold then only the sold tokens are issued and created. The ICO only issues the sold tokens and reduces the total token pool.

What makes a 'good' Token?

A token is considered a good token when the team issuing the token executes and delivers it accurately and perfectly.
