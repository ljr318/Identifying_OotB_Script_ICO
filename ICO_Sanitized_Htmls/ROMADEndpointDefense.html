Why classic antiviruses (AVs) donât work

Malware is evolving at an extremely fast pace: around 4 new malware samples are produced every second worldwide and there are over 750 million of them today.
How the classic AV combats malware?
When a new virus is detected, it is sent to the malware analytic laboratory. It means the attack has already taken place.
The malware analysts study the sample, make a signature for it and place it to the database. Sometimes this process takes a couple of hours, sometimes weeks.
The database finally gets to the endpoints. The endpoints should be protected from now on.
What are the disadvantages?
The protection is done post factum, when the malware has already caused irreparable damage.
While the protection is being developed, hundreds of new samples appear worldwide, meaning endpoints are actually never secure . WannaCry and NonPetya have confirmed that.

    BLOCKCHAIN

  • What problems does ROMAD solve with blockchain integration?

    ROMAD is to solve two problems.

    The first one is that ROMAD will make the repelled malware attacks data public to the whole industry. The depersonalized user data for the malware attack will be available for the whole cybersecurity industry in the transparent and authentic form â on the blockchain.

    The second one is to give the end users the ability to trade with their data. These days whenever you click on any software agreement, everyone is interested in your data. Google collects it. Facebook collects it. ROMAD will give its users the opportunity to regain the control of their own data and trade it on their own will without compromise of their privacy.

  • Why DAG blockchain, first of all?

    After a thorough research of all technology available, we conclude DAG is the optimum.

    As Ethereum literally eats HDDs these days, everyone starts to worry about the disk space. The DAG is the natural choice to eradicate these concerns. Each node sees only its own transactions when DAG is used.

    The obvious exception are the consensus nodes that must see the whole blockchain (or we better call it blockDAG) content. This is why there is a requirement for the delegate nodes to have enough space for the whole ROMAD blockDAG.

  • What happens if the delegate runs out of space?

    With technology development, the cost of hard disk is becoming cheaper and cheaper and the storage space is becoming larger and larger. For example, the price of 1TB HDD is only $50.

    Moreover, there are some optimization tricks we can refer to Bitcoin blockchain, such as Segregated Witness , or, for example, the threshold signatures to minimize the output. For more details, please refer to our comprehensive Github research at https://github.com/RomadHolding/

  • Why do you use dBFT?

    The Bitcoin blockchain gave the world the idea of the Proof based consensus. This was a Proof of Work (PoW). The further development had come up with more advanced ideas such as different Proof of Stake (PoS) consensuses. However, most of them are not BFT. There are hundreds of different blockchains and blockDAGs these days and there are very few of them (e.g. EOS, NEO, Ripple, Stellar, HashGraph) that actually attempt to combat the Byzantine Fault issue.

    There is a tradeoff between the platform speed and safety. BFT decreases the transactions speed for the sake of additional security (note, however, BFT is still much faster than PoW). Additional efforts are required to keep up the transactions rate high when BFT is active. For more details, please refer to our comprehensive Github research at https://github.com/RomadHolding/

  • Why do I want to have the delegate node?

    There is a reward in ROMAD tokens for running the delegate node. ROMAD uses the DASH concept (https://www.dash.org/masternodes/) for this. The profit calculator will be available later.

  • How can I owe the delegate node?

    Running a delegate node requires a VPS (Virtual Private Server) and a collateral of ROMAD tokens. Anyone who has the two, can have a delegate node. The number of the delegate nodes is limited. Initially ROMAD plans to have 100 delegate nodes.

As ROMAD analysts predicted, the less profitable the hidden mining becomes, the more profitable it gets to develop the hybrid malware. The hybrid malware is the one that combines multiple options to steal the digital assets.

L0rdix has appeared at the end of 2018. It is all-in-one, new Swiss army knife for the cybercrooks.
