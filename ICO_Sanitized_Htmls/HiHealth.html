CBD 101: What Exactly is CBD?

CBD is short for Cannabidiol. It’s pronounced “can-a-bi-di-ol”, and it represents one of 138 cannabinoids found in the Cannabis Sativa plant. Pure CBD makes up to 50% of the total plant. Along with another famous compound, THC, it is the most recognized compound found in cannabis. Whereas THC is derived from the marijuana plant CBD is extracted from the industrial hemp plant. They are cousins.

CBD provides a wide range of health benefits without making you high as it is a non-psychoactive compound. We, as humans, have an endocannabinoid system (ECS) and are uniquely designed to uptake and incorporate CBD into our physiological system. Cannabinoids are produced and exist within our bodies and sometimes at less than optimal levels. They can also be found in some foods that we eat. At times though the naturally occurring, and that which we get from foods is not enough. This is where CBD derived from hemp comes in. Taken in a tincture, capsule, vape, or mixed into foods it can help regain homeostatic balance.

How does CBD help
Endocannabinoid
System

Discovered in the 20th Century, our Endocannabinoid System (ECS) consists of numerous cannabinoid receptors that are endogenous to the mammalian brain, and play a pivotal role in physiological processes such as mood, memory, pain and appetite.

CB1 Receptors

CB1 receptors are primarily found in the brain and central nervous system, and to a lesser extent in other tissues.

CB2 Receptors

CB2 receptors are mostly in the peripheral organs especially cells associated with the immune system.

CBD Suppresses the breakdown of Anandamide, a naturally occurring Cannabinoid in the body that is responsible for activating the CB1 and CB2 receptors.
