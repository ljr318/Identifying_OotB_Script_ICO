Company
Centralized
  • Keeps profit

    Home-sharing companies, due to their business model, are unable to reward or offer discounts to their dedicated users. Hard-working hosts or frequent-traveling guests who have contributed to building the company from the ground up, are yet to receive a single gift or bonus for their input.

  • No community

    The original vision of the home-sharing industry was to help the guest feel like a local by integrating him in the community he travels to. However, this idea was quickly lost, due to our current business infrastructure where users are mere numbers and home-sharing is hardly different than hotels.

  • Controls communication

    Home-sharing companies do not permit the exchange of email, social media, video or phone calls, in fear that the users will bypass the platform and book in private. As a result guests often get deceived by distrustful hosts who oversell low quality rooms or even worse, the booking never existed in the first place. Hosts on the other hand, often accept guests they dislike, resulting in an unfortunate experience for both parties. Â

Guest
  • 10-20% fees

    Traditional home-sharing companies are responsible for acting as the trusted middleman for guests and hosts, thereby charging a high commission for their operating costs. The fees are mainly covered by the guest and are often shown only at the end, discouraging them to book the accommodation as they feel deceived. An added credit card fee is charged by the card issuer, making the overall process unnecessarily overpriced.

  • No dispute resolution

    When facing issues with a guest, the reporting and problem solving resolution in traditional home-sharing companies is long, slow and tedious. Hosts are demoralized to find solutions by reporting incidents and repeated disputes often discourage them from staying in this industry, as the costs and effort become overbearing.

  • CompanyControlsData

    Fear of the public being surveyed by private corporations has been on the rise, making users highly concerned about how their private information is handled. Companies keep hold of our data, exposing us to the risk of it being sold or shared with third parties for advertising and other purposes. In addition, there is also a possibility that centralized databases can get hacked, releasing our confidential information to unknown and unwarranted parties, with no control over their use of it.

  • 1.5% fees

    PopulStayâs decentralized nature is created by the trust blockchain technology provides, allowing it to act as the middleman, without the need for a third-centralized-party. Therefore, operating costs and fees are significantly decreased. Various payment methods are accepted from credit cards to several cryptocurrencies, consequently minimizing guestâs expenses and enhancing user experience for all parties.

  • Community jurors resolve disputes

    Blockchain technology allows the development of community-run, transparent, and fair dispute resolution applications. PopulStay is integrating such a platform to solve cases efficiently and for every booking, a deposit by both guest and host is held in escrow as collateral. If a dispute arises, evidence has to be presented to a randomly selected and qualified group of PopulStay jurors. The trialâs mechanics incentivize jurors to act fairly and the winning party will receive the aforementioned deposit as compensation.

  • Data ownership

    The blockchain allows us to securely store data with complete privacy and security on a distributed ledger. Thereby, the user is the only one with granted access to it and can decide who views it, for how long and for what purposes. When the need arises to KYP (Know Your Property) and KYC (Know Your Customer) as a host, you will be giving access only to the relevant data and will be immediately notified if it was shared or tampered with.

Software Platform

A cloud-based environment in which different PopulStay programs run on, and integrate seamlessly with blockchain technology.

Back-end

The backbone of our software platform, hosted on Amazon Web Services, provides us with security, accuracy, reliability and high performance.

Front-end

Our modern, intuitive web platform interacts with the PopulStay back-end to provide a smooth user experience while managing listings, booking properties, and all related functionalities of the ecosystem.

Mobile app

A free app for iOS & Android allows the user instant access to: bookings, customer support, host-guest messaging and more.

Blockchain

The real innovation that PopulStay brings to the market is the use of blockchain technology to manage listings, services, and devices. Blockchain is the next step in the evolution of information and value exchange across the Internet. No central authorities (like banks or corporations) are needed, as trusted transactions are exchanged among networks of independent computers.

Smart contracts

A set of enforced software instructions orchestrate the complex interactions between hosts-guests, service providers and IoT devices while providing an automated, secure and hassle-free experience. They perform the following ecosystem activities: bookings and listings, payments and refunds, external services, reviews and complaints, smart devices.

PPS Token

A customised blockchain token (PPS) is used to transfer value and information among the above components. Positive network effects take place, wherein token owners (hosts, guests and, third-parties) are incentivized to contribute to the ecosystemâs growth, thereby increasing the tokenâs value. Its use will also significantly decrease commission fees (up to 20%) by removing the need for intermediate central authorities, and allowing a far simpler, automated experience for hosts & guests.

Smart Devices

We envision a self-sustaining ecosystem, leveraging IoT and blockchain technology, in which the host needs minimal maintenance to manage listings while ensuring an unforgettable experience for the guests.

Smart locks

Our world-renowned Developers have designed secure, user-friendly locks which provide an unprecedented ease of use for our PopulStay hosts and guests. The integration with blockchain technology allows PIN codes to be generated (check-in) and revoked (check-out) automatically, resulting in a seamless experience for both parties.

Other IoT devices

We are developing an environment that satisfies guestsâ needs based on the preferences highlighted in their profile. At the same time, hosts can tailor device usage and limit excessive spending. Future integrations include: smart-WiFi, smart-TV, smart-fridges, smart-cars and, of course, current legacy IoT devices such as Alexa, Siri or Cortana.

The PopulStay Team

Walter Wang

Founder & CEO

Dr. Walter Wang, PhD graduate from NUS, has been a reputable Airbnb member since 2013 who has hosted over 500 guests. In 2015 he co-founded Igloohome, a company that created revolutionary smart-locks for the home sharing ecosystem. Their product won the CES Asia Innovation Award in 2017 & 2018 and was praised by Lee Hsien Loong, Prime Minister of Singapore as one of the most innovative products invented in the country. Inspired by blockchain technology, he went on to start his new venture, PopulStay, where he aims to re-ignite the sense of community that home sharing platforms have lost. He is also an avid runner and ukulele lover.
