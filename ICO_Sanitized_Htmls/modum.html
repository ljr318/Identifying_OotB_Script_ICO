To support our customers, we reliably create and provide innovative solutions based on trusted data to monitor, automate and optimize supply chains for sensitive goods, increasing quality while minimizing waste and inefficiencies.

Eager and passionate about solving our customers problems, we live by following key principles in everything we do: reliability & quality, customer success, trusted relationships, and agility & innovation.

It's Time to Change

Why Digitalizing Your Supply Chain Matters

Reduce the monitoring burden

Paper-based, manual process steps, unnecessary working capital costs and human effort are challenges which industries are still battling with today when it comes to monitoring their supply chain.

Our highly automated solutions are developed with usability in mind and support deviation-driven quality control to help you focus on what really matters.

Introduce new operating models based on trust

Supplier volatility, interoperability issues, privacy concerns – all these contribute to a lack of trust, making the exchange of information and the execution of processes inefficient and risky.

With strong process engineering skills and expertise in blockchain and machine learning, we are capable of building smart and permission-based decentralized network operating models.

Address regulatory requirements

The regulatory landscape is growing more complex and scrutiny with regards to quality requirements is increasing day by day across the globe.

We cut through the complexity by integrating risk management functionality into our fully qualified and highly secure solutions.

Minimize waste

Billions of dollars’ worth of food and pharmaceuticals are lost every year due to conditions not being met during transport. The impact is particularly severe in areas where shortages exist.

We build solutions that help you act rather than react by minimizing the risk of goods being damaged.

Seamless Tracking and Monitoring

Transparency and Control to the Consumer's Doorstep and Beyond

Today's supply chain networks include multiple stakeholders on a global scale, making information sharing complex as well as increasing the overall lack of trust and risk of quality issues along the chain.

But what if you had full transparency on the status of goods, right down to article level? What if you had a solution that tracks goods as well as its conditions centrally but is flexible to deal with different transportation stages? And what if you could include consumers in the process in a simple and accessible manner?

At Modum, we successfully collaborate with our customers to bring them closer to achieving this goal – one step at a time.

Applications

Addressing Challenges across Industries for every Stage of Distribution

Pharmaceuticals

Despite the strict regulations and stringent controls, 20% of temperature-controlled Pharma products are damaged every year due to the complexity of controlling a global distribution network.

Medical Devices

Medical Devices are often susceptible to shock and tilt. With the EU Medical Devices Regulation (MDR) in force, quality needs to be ensured along the supply chain on single unit level.

Perishable Foods and Beverages

Transport of perishable food is governed by strict regulations, such as IATA’s Perishable Cargo Regulations (PCR), which holds challenges for transports in hot and humid climates.

Construction Materials

Malleable construction materials can change their physical properties if environmental conditions are not adhered to, rendering them unsafe to use.

Primary Distribution

Intercontinental shipments of large volumes with multiple 3rd parties as well as various modes of transport involved require autonomous and adaptable monitoring solutions.

Last Mile

Large shipment volumes of small units sent to pharmacies or patients require a cost-effective and easy-to-use monitoring solution without any compromises on measuring and reporting.

Clinical Trials

Clinical Trials are a costly endeavor which requires seamless monitoring of transport conditions from Kit assembly to the patient as well as the ability to react immediately to deviations.

Personalized Medicine

A growing trend, personalized medicine will disrupt Pharma logistics and require more stringent monitoring due to its high unit value and short shelf-life.

We have successfully completed various digitalization projects with customers in various industries by adopting a process-focused approach to solving supply chain challenges and leveraging our own industry expertise as well as our connections to industry associations and academia. We build solutions that link the physical world of goods distribution with the digital world of shipment information and financial transactions, applying the latest technologies where it makes sense.
